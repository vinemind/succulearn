soil

Succulents and cacti take in a lot of moisture very quickly. To prevent moisture borne illness, these plants need soil with a lot of drainage. Regular potting soil does not provide adequate drainage, so your plant will need cactus/succulent soil.

Gardening stores sell cactus/succulent soil, but you can also mix it yourself. Gardening stores and construction stores will have the supplies you need. I personally mix my own soil, so Iâ€™ve provided the recipe I use.

Supplies Needed:

  • perlite
  • potting soil
  • sand

I use 3 parts potting soil, 3 parts sand, and 2 parts perlite. The measurements may vary depending on how much cactus/succulent soil you need. Mix/combine well before using with your plants.

Perlite absorbs excess water and improves drainage for your plant. Sand helps improve drainage. Both of these help make the soil less dense, therefore improving drainage.
