pots

The type of pot you use for your succulent matters.

Pots that have drainage holes allow for excess water to leave the pot, which greatly reduces the risk of your succulent drowning. It’s a safer bet to use a pot with a drainage hole if you’re new to caring for succulents. Pots with drainage holes typically come with a tray to catch the extra water. These pots can only be placed on a flat surface.

Pots that don’t have a drainage hole increase the risk of plant ailments from overwatering. Plants in these pots will have to be monitored closely for any signs of illness. These pots typically come in much more unique styles and can be displayed as a hanging object.
